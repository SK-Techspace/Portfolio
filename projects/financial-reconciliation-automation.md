# Financial Reconciliation Automation

## Business Problem

Financial reconciliation and month-end close activities were slowed by fragmented data sources, manual validation steps, and recurring billing discrepancies. Teams needed a more reliable and repeatable process to improve reporting accuracy and close timelines.

## Stakeholders

- Finance
- Reporting teams
- Data teams
- Business stakeholders

## Tools Used

- SQL
- Alteryx
- AWS S3
- Relational databases
- Excel

## Approach

I analyzed large financial datasets from AWS S3 and relational sources to identify the root causes of discrepancies in billing and invoicing. I then supported the design of automated reconciliation workflows using Alteryx and integrated them with P&L-related data to streamline recurring validation steps.

In parallel, I documented business requirements, facilitated alignment across stakeholder groups, and helped standardize validation and reporting practices so the solution could be adopted consistently by the broader team.

## Outcome

- Reduced **billing and invoicing discrepancies by 25%**
- Shortened **month-end close by 2 days**
- Improved reporting reliability and reconciliation consistency
- Supported on-time delivery across **4 analytics initiatives**

## Portfolio Relevance

This case study highlights both analytical problem solving and execution discipline: data investigation, automation, requirements gathering, process documentation, and change adoption.
